## 3. Manufacturing Levels Framework (1‚Äì5)
Introduce a clear 5-level global framework used throughout the hub. This is the backbone of filtering, PD design, and role mapping.

### 3.1. Levels Overview
Cards or grid displaying:

- **Level 1 ‚Äì Foundation & Safety**
  - Audience: New entrants, helpers, non-technical staff transitioning into manufacturing.
  - Focus: Safety, discipline, basic tools & measurements, industrial culture.
  - Outcomes: Safe work habits, basic shopfloor literacy.

- **Level 2 ‚Äì Operator & Technician**
  - Audience: Machine operators, basic maintenance techs, line workers.
  - Focus: SOPs, equipment checks, basic troubleshooting, 5S, communication.
  - Outcomes: Reliable operation, adherence to quality and safety standards.

- **Level 3 ‚Äì Advanced Technician & Specialist**
  - Audience: Experienced technicians, welding inspectors, quality technicians, production specialists.
  - Focus: advanced troubleshooting, RCA, data collection, lean projects.
  - Outcomes: Reduced downtime, improved quality, continuous improvement capability.

- **Level 4 ‚Äì Engineer & Supervisor**
  - Audience: Process/maintenance engineers, line leaders, supervisors.
  - Focus: process design & optimization, people leadership, OEE, cost management.
  - Outcomes: Higher throughput, optimized lines, better team performance.

- **Level 5 ‚Äì Manager & Industrial Leader**
  - Audience: Plant managers, operations managers, industrial leaders.
  - Focus: strategy, digital transformation, risk, financial decision-making.
  - Outcomes: Competitive, profitable, Industry 4.0-ready operations.

### 3.2. Level ‚Üí Typical Roles
Include a short mapping by level:

- Level 1: Helper, Entry Operator, Shopfloor Trainee
- Level 2: Operator, Mechanical Technician, Electrical Technician, Production Cell Operator
- Level 3: Senior Technician, Welding Specialist, Quality Technician, Reliability Tech
- Level 4: Process Engineer, Quality Engineer, Maintenance Supervisor, Production Supervisor
- Level 5: Plant Manager, Operations Director, Industrial Transformation Leader
