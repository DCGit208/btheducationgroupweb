## Official definitions

### SWI Partner — Strategic Workforce Infrastructure Partner

Executive-level institutional participant responsible for governance, ecosystem stewardship, divisional oversight, workforce infrastructure expansion, and strategic coordination.

**Source:** `Strategic Workforce Infrastructure (SWI) Partner Definition.docx`, `STRATEGIC WORKFORCE INFRASTRUCTURE (SWI) PARTNER ONBOARDING & GOVERNANCE MANUAL.docx`

### SWD-INV — Strategic Workforce Development Infrastructure Participant

Participant responsible for workforce expansion, workforce portfolio development, institutional coordination, deployment continuity, and workforce infrastructure scaling.

**NOT an investor.**

**Source:** `SWD-INV Institutional Collaborator Program.docx`, GORP Framework Agreement

### WDS — Workforce Development Structure

Operational workforce development structure responsible for workforce production, deployment readiness, continuity, cohort operations, and performance supervision.

**Source:** `DEPLOYMENT & WORKFORCE OPERATIONS MANUAL.docx`

### WDDAM

**Develop · Deploy · Acquire · Govern**

Workforce lifecycle architecture governing production, deployment, acquisition, and governance of deployable professional capacity.

WDDAM is **not** training, recruitment, or staffing. It is workforce infrastructure.

## Four workforce infrastructure engines

| Engine | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Development** | Workforce production economics — certification pathways, cohort structuring, operational readiness |
| **Deployment** | Operational workforce continuity — placement, contracts, retention |
| **Acquisition** | Institutional workforce transfer — acquisition rights, continuity agreements |
| **Governance** | Lifecycle oversight — performance, compliance, reporting, continuity |

Frame as **recurring workforce infrastructure economics**. Never as investment returns.

**Development Engine values above = Engine 01 (Development) alone** for modeled workforce production economics. Deployment, Acquisition, and Governance (Engines 02–04) accrue as **additional lifecycle economics** beyond this baseline — the same workforce asset generates value across all four engines over its lifecycle.

**Source:** `PORTFOLIO ECONOMICS APPENDIX.docx` §3, Operations Manual §2
